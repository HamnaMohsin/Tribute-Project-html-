Malcolm X

A fearless voice for justice, equality, and Black empowerment.

Malcolm X (1925–1965) was a prominent African-American Muslim minister and human rights activist. He was a courageous advocate for the rights of Black Americans and is considered one of the most influential figures in the fight against racial injustice in the United States.

Early Life

Malcolm X was born Malcolm Little on May 19, 1925, in Omaha, Nebraska. After facing many personal struggles and injustices in his youth, he became involved in criminal activity and was eventually imprisoned. While in prison, he educated himself and converted to Islam.

  • Born Malcolm Little
  • Faced racial discrimination in early life
  • Imprisoned in his 20s
  • Converted to Islam in prison

Activism and Beliefs

Malcolm X became a minister and national spokesperson for the Nation of Islam. He advocated for Black empowerment, self-defense, and pride. His speeches and writings challenged the status quo and inspired many to fight for their dignity and rights.

  • Spokesperson for Nation of Islam
  • Promoted Black pride and unity
  • Encouraged self-reliance and self-defense
  • Challenged racism and inequality through powerful speeches

Later Years and Legacy

In 1964, Malcolm X left the Nation of Islam and made a pilgrimage to Mecca. This experience changed his outlook, leading him to embrace a more inclusive approach to civil rights. He founded the Organization of Afro-American Unity before being tragically assassinated on February 21, 1965, in New York City.

  • Made a pilgrimage to Mecca (Hajj)
  • Embraced global brotherhood and unity
  • Founded the Organization of Afro-American Unity (OAAU)
  • Assassinated in 1965

Famous Quote

"Education is the passport to the future, for tomorrow belongs to those who prepare for it today." – Malcolm X
